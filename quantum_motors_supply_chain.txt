Company Name: Quantum Motors
Ticker: QMOT
Industry: Electric Vehicles & Autonomous Driving

Headquarters:
Quantum Motors HQ is located in Austin, Texas. This is where all the vehicle design, software engineering, and final AI safety testing occurs. We require high-performance AI chips, heavy-duty batteries, and lidar sensors shipped directly here for integration before vehicles are sent to customers.

Tier 1 Suppliers (Final Assembly & Core Components):
1. **Quantum Gigafactory Berlin** (Berlin, Germany):
   - This massive facility handles the final assembly of our flagship European models.
   - They need constant shipments of forged steel chassis components and electric motors.
   - They supply fully assembled "Euro-Spec EVs" directly to our Austin HQ for final global distribution logging.

2. **VoltCore Power Systems** (Seoul, South Korea):
   - VoltCore is our primary supplier for next-generation solid-state batteries.
   - They ship battery packs directly to Austin HQ and to the Berlin Gigafactory.

3. **NeuroVision Semiconductors** (Hsinchu, Taiwan):
   - NeuroVision manufactures our custom autonomous driving "Q-Brain" AI chips.
   - These highly sensitive chips are shipped directly to Austin HQ.

4. **LumiSense Technologies** (Kyoto, Japan):
   - LumiSense provides advanced LiDAR and optical sensor arrays.
   - They ship sensors to Austin HQ and also to Berlin.

Tier 2 Suppliers (Raw Materials & Sub-components):
5. **Titan Forge Industries** (Monterrey, Mexico):
   - Titan Forge handles all the heavy metal stamping and creates the forged steel chassis.
   - They ship these heavy chassis components directly to the Berlin Gigafactory for assembly.

6. **Lithium Dynamics** (Antofagasta, Chile):
   - Lithium Dynamics is responsible for mining and refining high-grade lithium.
   - They ship raw lithium material across the ocean to VoltCore Power Systems in Seoul.

7. **RareEarth Extraction Co.** (Baotou, China):
   - They mine neodymium and other rare earth magnets crucial for our electric motors.
   - These magnetic components are shipped to the Berlin Gigafactory where the motors are finalized.
